## Email Copy Best Practices

### Subject Lines
1. **Keep under 50 characters** (mobile preview)
2. **Be specific, not generic** ("Does your board need a vote?" not "HOA Update")
3. **Personalize when possible** ([First Name], [HOA Name], [project type])
4. **No jargon** (avoid "capital improvements", say "roof replacement")
5. **Create value, not curiosity** (helpful, not clickbait)

### Email Body
1. **Respect their time** â€” busy board volunteers and PMs
2. **Short paragraphs** (2-3 sentences max)
3. **One primary CTA** (application OR free consult)
4. **Specific numbers** (not "save money", use "$8,500 special assessment")
5. **No jargon** (explain terms like "AR aging" if you use them)
6. **Always include unsubscribe** (CAN-SPAM compliance)
7. **Make it easy to reply** (conversational tone, invite questions)

### Personalization

Use merge tags where available:
- `[First Name]` â€” in subject and greeting
- `[HOA Name]` â€” if provided in form or consult
- `[Project Type]` â€” if known (roof, pool, siding, etc.)
